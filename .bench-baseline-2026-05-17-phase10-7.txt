=== Phase 10.7 surface baseline (mindc 0.2.11, 2026-05-17) ===

Re-baseline after Phase 10.7 closed the match-and-reference line on
top of Phase 10.6's RFN-side surface. The parser's hot path now pays
for two more checks per atom: `at(b'&')` (single byte compare, ref-
take prefix) and `at(b'm') && at_keyword(b"match")` (two-stage match-
keyword fast-path). Both arms exit in O(1) when the leading byte does
not match; the cost is per-atom and additive.

Additionally `canonicalize_module` now invokes `module_has_reap_threshold`
(O(n) one-pass scan over IR instrs). For non-MoE modules this skips
the REAP DCE pass entirely; the scan itself is a few matches!
checks. Verified zero overhead on small_matmul, ~4% on medium_mlp,
~8% on large_network — the larger inputs amortize the per-atom adds
across more parse-primary calls.

Frozen reference measurement (CI Ubuntu 24.04, `cargo bench --bench
compiler -- --warm-up-time 3 --measurement-time 8 --output-format bencher`):

  small_matmul:   2.81 µs   (was 2.81 µs at Phase 10.6 baseline → +0.0%)
  medium_mlp:     6.56 µs   (was 6.31 µs at Phase 10.6 baseline → +4.0%)
  large_network: 16.90 µs   (was 15.70 µs at Phase 10.6 baseline → +7.6%)

The medium_mlp delta sits below the existing 5% gate; the large_network
delta is what triggers the re-baseline. Both are inside the
microsecond-class compile envelope — total pipeline time for the
largest pre-built network is still under 17 µs.

Gate threshold remains 5% from this baseline. Future commits cannot
exceed 5% over these numbers without an explicit re-baseline + sign-off
documented here. CI runner-to-runner variance on `ubuntu-latest`
empirically tracks within ±2%, leaving 3% headroom inside the gate.

Phase 10.7 micro-optimizations landed in the same commit:
  - `parse_primary` now does `at(b'm') && at_keyword(b"match")` rather
    than the bare 5-byte keyword check. Single-byte short-circuit on
    the dominant non-match case.

No further parser fast-paths are obvious without disturbing the
legibility of the Pratt loop. The next round of optimization, if
needed, would target `canonicalize_module` by caching the result of
`module_has_reap_threshold` during IR construction (set a bool on
`IRModule` at lower time).
